Finance Policy Document

Topic: Financial Controls and Anti-Fraud Policy

Details:
The company maintains robust financial controls to prevent errors, fraud, and misuse of company funds.

KEY FINANCIAL CONTROLS:
1. Segregation of Duties: No single employee controls an entire financial transaction
   - Raising PO ≠ person approving PO ≠ person receiving goods
   - Payroll input ≠ payroll approval ≠ payroll payment authorization
   - Invoice processing ≠ payment authorization

2. Dual Authorization:
   - All payments over AED 10,000: Two authorized signatories required
   - Bank transfers: Finance + CFO dual approval in banking system
   - No single person can make large payments unilaterally

3. Approval Hierarchies:
   - Documented and enforced in Finance Portal
   - System enforces approval at each stage — payment cannot proceed without full approval chain

4. System Access Controls:
   - Finance system (ERP) access restricted to roles that need it
   - View-only access for budget holders who need to monitor budgets
   - No generic or shared logins in Finance systems

5. Audit Trails:
   - All Finance system transactions logged with user, timestamp, and action
   - Logs reviewed monthly by Finance Manager
   - Logs retained for 7 years (legal requirement)

FRAUD RISK AREAS AND PREVENTION:
Expense claim fraud: Prevent by requiring original VAT invoices and manager approval
Payroll fraud (ghost employees): Prevent by HR-Finance reconciliation of headcount monthly
Vendor fraud: Prevent by vendor screening, PO requirement, 3-way match
Corporate card fraud: Prevent by monthly reconciliation and purchase monitoring
Kickbacks: Prevent by conflict of interest declarations and segregation of duties

REPORTING SUSPECTED FRAUD:
- Immediately report to: Internal Audit Manager or ethics@company.com
- Anonymous reporting: Ethics Portal (accessible without login)
- Do not confront the suspected person yourself
- Do not tell others about your suspicion (may alert perpetrator)
- Whistleblower protection: Full protection from retaliation guaranteed by company policy and UAE law

CONSEQUENCES OF FRAUD:
- Immediate suspension pending investigation
- Termination on finding of fraud
- Recovery of all fraudulently obtained funds (through legal means including UAE courts)
- Criminal prosecution: UAE Penal Code provisions apply to workplace fraud and embezzlement

FINANCIAL DELEGATION OF AUTHORITY:
- Full Document of Authority (DOA) available: Finance Portal → Policies → Delegation of Authority
- Specifies: Who can approve what amount, for which types of transactions
- Must be followed — bypassing approval thresholds is a disciplinary offence
