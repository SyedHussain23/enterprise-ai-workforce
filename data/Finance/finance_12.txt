Finance Policy Document

Topic: Procurement and Purchase Order Policy

Details:
All company purchases must follow the procurement policy to ensure value for money and proper authorization.

PROCUREMENT PRINCIPLES:
- Best value: Not always the cheapest — consider quality, reliability, service, and total cost of ownership
- Competitive bidding: Multiple quotes required for significant purchases
- No conflict of interest: Employees must not purchase from companies they have a financial interest in
- Sustainability: Prefer suppliers with environmental and ethical credentials

PURCHASE THRESHOLDS AND APPROVAL:
- Under AED 500: Employee purchase, manager approval via expense claim (no PO needed)
- AED 500–5,000: Department head approval, PO required
- AED 5,001–25,000: Department head + Finance Manager approval, minimum 2 quotes required
- AED 25,001–100,000: Finance Director approval, minimum 3 quotes required (Request for Quotation — RFQ)
- Over AED 100,000: CFO + CEO approval, formal Request for Proposal (RFP) process required

HOW TO RAISE A PURCHASE ORDER (PO):
1. Finance Portal → Procurement → New Purchase Order
2. Select supplier from approved vendor list (or request new vendor registration first)
3. Enter: Item description, quantity, unit price, cost center, delivery date
4. Attach: Supplier quote/proposal
5. Submit for approval (routed automatically per approval thresholds)
6. PO number assigned on approval — share with supplier
7. Supplier must reference PO number on their invoice

VENDOR REGISTRATION:
- New suppliers must complete vendor onboarding before any purchase
- Finance Portal → Vendors → Register New Vendor
- Required: Trade license, TRN, bank details, insurance certificate
- Vendor screening conducted by Finance (anti-bribery compliance check)
- Approved vendor added to supplier list within 5–7 business days

THREE-WAY MATCH:
- Finance processes payment only after 3-way match:
  1. Purchase Order (what was ordered)
  2. Goods/Service Receipt (what was actually delivered)
  3. Invoice (what supplier is charging)
- All three must match before payment released

PREFERRED VENDOR LIST:
- Pre-negotiated contracts with preferred vendors for common purchases (stationery, IT equipment, catering)
- Using preferred vendors: Faster process, no RFQ required
- Preferred vendor list: Finance Portal → Procurement → Preferred Vendors

EMERGENCY PROCUREMENT:
- Urgent purchase without time for normal PO process (genuine emergency only)
- Verbal approval from Finance Manager or CFO first
- Retroactive PO submitted within 24 hours with explanation
- Abuse of emergency procurement: Disciplinary action
