Finance Policy Document

Topic: UAE Corporate Tax and Regulatory Compliance

Details:
Key information about UAE tax obligations relevant to employees and business operations.

UAE TAX ENVIRONMENT:
- Personal income tax: NONE in UAE (major benefit of working in UAE)
- Corporate tax: 9% on business profits above AED 375,000 (from June 2023)
- VAT: 5% (from January 2018)
- Customs duty: 5% on most imported goods

PERSONAL TAX — EMPLOYEE PERSPECTIVE:
- Employees pay NO income tax in UAE on UAE-sourced income
- No tax deductions from salary — employees receive full gross package
- No tax filing required by employees for UAE income
- Exception: Citizens/residents of countries with worldwide income taxation (US citizens, UK domiciled individuals) — these employees are personally responsible for their home country tax filings on global income
- Company provides salary certificates for tax filing purposes in home countries on request

CORPORATE TAX — BUSINESS IMPACT (Not employee-facing):
- Companies earning over AED 375,000 profit/year pay 9% corporate tax
- Free zone companies: May benefit from 0% rate if meeting Qualifying Free Zone criteria
- Finance team handles all corporate tax compliance
- Employees should not make representations about company tax status to clients

VAT OBLIGATIONS FOR EMPLOYEES:
- Always get proper VAT invoice from suppliers when buying anything for the company
- VAT invoice must show: Supplier TRN, VAT amount separately, total
- Without proper VAT invoice: Company cannot reclaim VAT, making purchase 5% more expensive
- Do not accept just a payment receipt — insist on tax invoice

DOUBLE TAXATION AGREEMENTS (DTAs):
- UAE has DTAs with 130+ countries
- DTAs prevent double taxation for residents with foreign income
- Relevant for: Foreign-source investment income, rental income in home country
- Consult personal tax advisor for individual circumstances — company HR provides certificate of employment/tax residency for DTA claims

UAE ECONOMIC SUBSTANCE REGULATIONS:
- Certain business activities must demonstrate real economic presence in UAE
- Finance team manages ESR compliance
- Employees should not be registered as directors of shell companies (potential ESR risk)

COMMON EMPLOYEE TAX QUESTIONS:
Q: Do I pay tax on my UAE salary? A: No — UAE has no personal income tax
Q: Do I need to file a tax return in UAE? A: No — no personal tax returns required in UAE
Q: What about tax in my home country? A: Depends on your home country law — consult personal tax advisor
Q: Can company provide a tax residency certificate? A: Yes — request via HR Portal → My Documents → Tax Residency Certificate
