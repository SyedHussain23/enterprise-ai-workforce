Topic: UAE VAT Policy and Compliance

Details:
The UAE introduced Value Added Tax (VAT) at 5% from 1 January 2018 under Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017.

VAT BASICS FOR EMPLOYEES:
- UAE VAT rate: 5% (one of the lowest in the world)
- UAE has NO personal income tax — VAT is a consumption tax on goods and services
- VAT registered businesses collect VAT on behalf of Federal Tax Authority (FTA)
- Company is VAT registered (TRN: provided in Finance Portal)

IMPACT ON EMPLOYEES:
- Expense claims: VAT on business expenses is reclaimable — always get VAT invoice from suppliers
- Client invoices: All company invoices to clients include 5% VAT
- Salary: Not subject to VAT — this is a direct payment, not a supply of goods/services

VAT-EXEMPT EXPENSES (No VAT charged):
- Bare land sale/lease
- Local passenger transport
- Residential property (first supply)
- Certain healthcare services
- Certain education services

ZERO-RATED ITEMS (VAT at 0%):
- International transport
- Exports of goods outside GCC
- Oil and gas
- Investment-grade precious metals
- Certain food items

HOW TO ENSURE CORRECT VAT TREATMENT ON EXPENSE CLAIMS:
1. Always request a proper VAT invoice (not just a receipt)
2. VAT invoice must include: Supplier TRN, date, description, subtotal, VAT amount, total
3. Upload VAT invoice in Finance Portal → Expense Claims → Attach Invoice
4. Without valid VAT invoice: VAT amount cannot be reclaimed — Finance may only reimburse net amount

VAT INVOICES REQUIREMENTS:
Company VAT invoices must include:
- Company name and TRN (Tax Registration Number)
- Client name and TRN (if B2B)
- Invoice date and number
- Description of goods/services
- Net amount, VAT amount (5%), total amount
- Finance team prepares all client invoices — do not create invoices yourself

CORPORATE TAX (CTE):
- UAE introduced corporate tax from June 2023 (9% on profits above AED 375,000)
- This affects company finances but NOT individual employee salaries
- Employees do not file personal corporate tax returns in UAE

COMMON EMPLOYEE VAT QUESTIONS:
Q: Is my salary subject to VAT? A: No — employee salaries are not VATable
Q: Can I claim VAT back on personal expenses? A: No — only business expenses via company
Q: Do I need a TRN? A: No — TRN is for the company, not individuals
