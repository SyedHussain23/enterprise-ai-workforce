Finance Policy Document

Topic: Performance-Related Pay and Salary Review Process

Details:
How salary increases are determined and the annual review process.

ANNUAL SALARY REVIEW CYCLE:
- Review period: January–March each year
- Effective date of salary changes: 1st April annually
- Process:
  1. Performance ratings finalized (November–December)
  2. Finance provides salary bands and budget for each department (January)
  3. Managers submit salary recommendations within budget (February)
  4. HR and Finance validate against: Salary bands, market data, internal equity (February–March)
  5. CHRO and CFO final approval (March)
  6. Salary letters issued: 31st March (new salary effective 1st April payroll)

SALARY INCREASE GUIDELINES (Based on performance rating):
- Rating 5 (Outstanding): 10–20% increase
- Rating 4 (Exceeds Expectations): 7–12% increase
- Rating 3 (Meets Expectations): 3–6% increase
- Rating 2 (Below Expectations): 0–2% (may be withheld if performance plan active)
- Rating 1 (Unsatisfactory): No increase

Note: Increases are guidelines, not guarantees. Budget constraints may affect the final amount.

MARKET SALARY BENCHMARKING:
- Finance and HR use third-party salary surveys (Mercer, Hay Group, Gulf Compensation Forum)
- Benchmarking ensures salaries are competitive for UAE/GCC market
- Target positioning: Median (50th percentile) for standard performers; 75th percentile for exceptional performers
- If your salary is below market median: Flagged for out-of-cycle review

OUT-OF-CYCLE SALARY REVIEWS:
- Permitted for: Counter-offers (retention risk), promotions, significant role changes, market realignment
- Requires: Department Head + HR Director + CFO approval
- Maximum once per 12-month period per employee
- Counter-offer consideration: Company policy is to retain talent but not engage in bidding wars

SALARY COMPRESSION AND EQUITY:
- Finance monitors: Junior employee salaries must not exceed or approach senior employee salaries without justification
- New hire salaries: Checked against internal peers before offer (Finance + HR)
- Any internal equity issue identified: Actioned in next annual review or sooner if significant

COMMUNICATING THE INCREASE:
- Salary letter issued personally to each employee
- Discuss with your manager — do not discuss salary publicly with colleagues
- Salary information is confidential — sharing with others is a disciplinary matter
- Questions about your increase: Speak with your HR partner
