Finance Policy Document

Topic: Salary and Payroll Policy

Details:
The company pays all employees on time and in compliance with the UAE Wage Protection System (WPS).

SALARY PAYMENT:
- Payment date: Last working day of each month
- Method: Bank transfer via UAE WPS (mandatory for all private sector companies)
- Currency: UAE Dirhams (AED) unless employment contract specifies otherwise
- Payslips: Available on HR Portal → Payroll → My Payslips by the 25th of each month

SALARY STRUCTURE (Typical breakdown):
- Basic Salary: 40–60% of total package (used for gratuity, overtime, and leave encashment calculations)
- Housing Allowance: 20–30% of total package
- Transport Allowance: 10–15% of total package
- Other Allowances: Communication, education (role/grade specific)
- Total package = all above components combined

PAYSLIP CONTENTS:
- Employee name, ID, department, grade
- Month and year
- All earnings itemized (basic, allowances)
- All deductions itemized (advance repayments, absences, loans)
- Net pay amount
- Year-to-date totals

HOW TO ACCESS YOUR PAYSLIP:
1. Login to HR Portal → Payroll → My Payslips
2. Select month and year
3. Download PDF copy for records
4. Annual salary certificates available by 31st January each year

SALARY DISCREPANCIES:
- If you believe your salary is incorrect, raise via HR Portal → Payroll → Salary Query
- All salary queries submitted by 20th of the month will be corrected in same month's payroll
- Queries submitted after 20th: Corrected in following month's payroll
- Overpayments: Finance will recover in installments (not one lump deduction)

WPS COMPLIANCE:
- UAE law mandates salary payment within 10 days of agreed payment date
- Non-payment triggers Ministry of Human Resources (MOHRE) investigation
- Company is fully WPS compliant — all salaries routed through UAE banking system
- No salary paid in cash (except petty cash reimbursements per policy)

UAE — NO PERSONAL INCOME TAX:
- UAE does not have personal income tax — employees receive full gross salary
- No PAYE, no tax deductions from salary
- Employees from countries with worldwide income taxation (e.g., US citizens) are responsible for their own tax obligations in home country

BANK ACCOUNT REQUIREMENTS:
- UAE bank account required for salary receipt (IBAN format)
- Account must be in employee's own name
- Accepted banks: Emirates NBD, FAB, ADCB, DIB, Mashreq, RAK Bank, ENBD, HSBC UAE
- Submit IBAN within first 5 days of joining (HR Portal → My Profile → Bank Details)
