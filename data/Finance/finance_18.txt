Finance Policy Document

Topic: Cash Flow Management and Treasury Policy

Details:
The company manages cash flow proactively to ensure sufficient liquidity for all operational needs.

CASH FLOW OBJECTIVES:
- Maintain minimum 90-day operating expense reserve at all times
- Collect client payments promptly to minimize Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
- Manage supplier payment terms to optimize working capital
- Minimize idle cash through short-term investment where appropriate

ACCOUNTS RECEIVABLE (COLLECTING FROM CLIENTS):
- Payment terms on all client invoices: Net 30 days (standard)
- Invoice issued within 5 business days of service delivery
- Automated payment reminders: 7 days before due, on due date, 7 days overdue
- Collection escalation:
  * 0–30 days overdue: Automated reminder + AR team follow-up
  * 30–60 days: Account Manager escalates with client relationship awareness
  * 60–90 days: Finance Director and relevant Director jointly contact client
  * Over 90 days: Legal review for debt recovery

EMPLOYEE ROLE IN CASH COLLECTION:
- Account managers and project leads must ensure clients are invoiced promptly
- Do not agree to extended payment terms without Finance Director approval
- Report any client indicating payment difficulty immediately to Finance and your manager
- Do not promise credit notes or payment deferrals to clients — Finance must approve

ACCOUNTS PAYABLE (PAYING SUPPLIERS):
- Pay within agreed terms (typically Net 30)
- Early payment discounts evaluated case-by-case (often worth taking if company has cash)
- Do not promise suppliers early payment without Finance approval

FOREIGN CURRENCY:
- Company operates primarily in AED (pegged to USD at AED 3.67)
- GCC currencies: Generally stable, low FX risk
- International contracts in other currencies: Finance hedges FX exposure on significant contracts
- Employee expenses incurred abroad: Reimbursed at exchange rate at time of transaction (Finance Portal uses live FX rates)
- Claiming foreign currency expenses: Enter in original currency in Finance Portal (auto-converts to AED)

BANKING RELATIONSHIPS:
- Primary bank: Emirates NBD (operating account)
- Secondary bank: First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB)
- Treasury investments: Conservative — money market funds, UAE government bonds
- No speculative investments with company operating funds

CASH ADVANCE FOR EMPLOYEES:
- For travel cash advance: See Travel Policy (Finance_8)
- For personal advance: See Salary Advance Policy (Finance_11)
- All cash advances tracked in Finance system and must be reconciled promptly
