HR Policy Document

Topic: Anti-Harassment and Workplace Dignity Policy

Details:
The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward all forms of harassment, discrimination, and bullying.

DEFINITION OF HARASSMENT:
Sexual harassment: Unwanted conduct of a sexual nature — verbal (comments, jokes), non-verbal (gestures, inappropriate messages), or physical (touching without consent).

Workplace bullying: Persistent, unreasonable behavior directed at an employee that causes physical or psychological harm — shouting, belittling, spreading rumors, exclusion from meetings.

Discrimination: Treating someone unfairly based on: gender, nationality, religion, age, marital status, disability, pregnancy, ethnicity, or any protected characteristic.

EXAMPLES OF UNACCEPTABLE BEHAVIOR:
- Making derogatory comments about someone's nationality, accent, or appearance
- Sending inappropriate jokes or images via email, WhatsApp, or Teams
- Repeatedly interrupting or talking over colleagues in meetings
- Excluding team members from social events or meetings based on personal biases
- Making threats about someone's job security to intimidate
- Requesting romantic relationships as a condition of employment or career advancement

HOW TO REPORT HARASSMENT:
Option 1: Report to your direct manager (if not the perpetrator)
Option 2: Report to HR Partner directly (hr@company.com)
Option 3: Anonymous Ethics Hotline: ethics@company.com
Option 4: Direct report to HR Director for cases involving management

ALL REPORTS ARE TREATED CONFIDENTIALLY.

INVESTIGATION PROCESS:
- Investigation begins within 5 business days of report
- Interim protective measures in place (e.g., temporary separation of parties)
- Both parties interviewed separately
- Witnesses interviewed confidentially
- Written outcome with decision and consequences within 15 business days
- Appeal process available within 10 days of outcome

CONSEQUENCES FOR PERPETRATORS:
- Minor harassment: Written warning + mandatory training
- Moderate harassment: Final warning + suspension
- Serious harassment: Immediate termination (Article 44, UAE Labour Law)
- Criminal behavior (physical assault, sexual harassment): Police report filed

VICTIM SUPPORT:
- Immediate access to Employee Assistance Program (EAP) — free counseling
- Transfer option available to protect victim (perpetrator relocated, not victim)
- HR monitors situation post-investigation for minimum 6 months

UAE LEGAL CONTEXT:
- UAE Federal Penal Code includes provisions against sexual harassment and assault
- UAE Federal Law No. 2 of 2015 on combating discrimination and hatred
- Employer has legal duty to provide safe working environment
