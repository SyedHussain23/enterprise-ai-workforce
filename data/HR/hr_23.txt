HR Policy Document

Topic: Dress Code and Appearance Policy

Details:
The company maintains a professional dress code aligned with UAE business culture and industry standards.

GENERAL PRINCIPLES:
- Attire should be professional, neat, and appropriate for a business environment
- UAE cultural sensitivities must be respected at all times
- Clothing must not contain offensive slogans, political statements, or inappropriate imagery
- Clean, well-maintained, and ironed clothing expected at all times

BUSINESS FORMAL (Required for: client meetings, presentations, senior leadership interactions):
- Men: Formal trousers, collared dress shirt, jacket/blazer, formal shoes
- Women: Formal trousers or skirt (knee-length minimum), formal blouse or suit jacket, closed-toe shoes or conservative heels
- National dress: UAE Kandura and Ghutrah / Abaya are fully accepted and welcomed

BUSINESS CASUAL (Standard office dress code):
- Men: Chinos or smart trousers, collared polo or button shirt, leather shoes or smart loafers. No jeans, no trainers, no shorts
- Women: Smart trousers, professional dresses (covering shoulders and knees), blouses, blazers, smart flats
- Denim: Smart dark jeans acceptable (no tears, patches, or distressing)

CASUAL FRIDAY:
- Smart casual permitted on Fridays
- Jeans (neat, dark) permitted
- Clean casual shoes acceptable (no flip flops)
- Still no shorts, revealing tops, or gym wear

ROLES WITH UNIFORM:
- Operations, security, facilities: Company uniform provided and must be worn in full
- Replacement uniforms available from HR Facilities team

WHAT IS NOT PERMITTED:
- Shorts (above knee) in any area of office
- Sleeveless tops or off-shoulder clothing (in common areas)
- Flip flops or beach footwear
- Visible undergarments
- Excessively tight or sheer clothing
- Gym / activewear in meetings or client areas

PERSONAL GROOMING:
- Hair: Well-groomed and clean; extreme or unnatural colors must not be visible to clients
- Tattoos: Should be covered in client-facing environments (arms and neck)
- Perfume: Modest application (considerate of colleagues with sensitivities)
- Nails: Clean and professional

DRESS CODE DURING RAMADAN:
- Extra modesty expected by all employees during Ramadan month, out of cultural respect
- Arms and legs should be covered in the office

Dress code violations: First instance — gentle reminder by manager. Repeat violations — formal warning.
