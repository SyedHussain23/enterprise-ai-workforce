HR Policy Document

Topic: Disciplinary Procedure

Details:
The company applies a fair and consistent disciplinary procedure to address employee misconduct and performance issues.

GROUNDS FOR DISCIPLINARY ACTION:
Minor offences:
- Repeated late arrival
- Excessive personal calls/internet use
- Not following reasonable management instructions
- Poor timekeeping or attendance

Moderate offences:
- Unauthorized absence
- Failure to meet performance targets without valid reason
- Breach of company policies (dress code, safety rules)
- Inappropriate conduct with colleagues

Serious offences:
- Theft or fraud (immediate termination)
- Harassment, bullying, or discrimination
- Violence or threat of violence in the workplace
- Gross insubordination
- Misuse or disclosure of confidential company information
- Competing with the employer
- Working for a competitor without disclosure

DISCIPLINARY STAGES:

Stage 1 — Verbal Warning:
- Given by direct manager for minor first offence
- Documented in HR system
- Remains on record for 6 months (then removed if no recurrence)

Stage 2 — Written Warning:
- Issued after second offence or more serious breach
- Employee may respond in writing within 5 days
- Remains on record for 12 months

Stage 3 — Final Written Warning:
- Given for repeated misconduct or serious offence
- Specific improvement targets set with timeline
- Remains on record for 18 months

Stage 4 — Termination:
- After exhausting warnings or for gross misconduct
- UAE Labour Law Article 44 permits immediate termination without notice or gratuity for:
  * Fraud or impersonation
  * Causing financial damage to employer
  * Drunk on duty
  * Physical assault of employer or colleagues
  * Disclosing trade secrets
  * Absent without leave for 7+ days in a year

DISCIPLINARY INVESTIGATION PROCESS:
1. Manager reports incident to HR with written account
2. HR suspends employee (with pay) pending investigation for serious offences
3. Investigation committee formed (HR, manager's manager, neutral party)
4. Employee given opportunity to present their case
5. Decision made within 10 working days
6. Decision communicated in writing
7. Right to appeal within 7 days of receiving decision

EMPLOYEE RIGHTS DURING DISCIPLINARY:
- Right to be accompanied by a colleague to any disciplinary meeting
- Right to see all evidence presented against them
- Right to respond before any decision is made
- Right to appeal any decision
