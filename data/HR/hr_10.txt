HR Policy Document

Topic: Grievance Policy and Employee Complaint Procedure

Details:
The company is committed to resolving workplace concerns fairly, promptly, and confidentially.

WHAT IS A GRIEVANCE:
A grievance is any concern, problem, or complaint an employee has related to:
- Working conditions or environment
- Management behavior or treatment
- Discrimination, harassment, or bullying
- Unfair treatment in performance review or promotion
- Salary discrepancy or payroll errors
- Breach of company policy by another employee
- Any other workplace matter affecting wellbeing

INFORMAL RESOLUTION (First Step):
Most grievances are best resolved through open conversation:
1. Raise concern directly with the person involved (if safe and appropriate)
2. If unsuccessful, discuss with direct manager
3. Manager must acknowledge and respond within 5 business days
4. Resolution agreement documented in writing

FORMAL GRIEVANCE PROCESS:
If informal resolution fails:
Step 1: Submit formal Grievance Form via HR Portal → Employee Relations → Raise Grievance
Step 2: HR acknowledges receipt within 2 business days
Step 3: HR appoints an independent HR partner to investigate
Step 4: Investigation includes interviews with all relevant parties (confidential)
Step 5: Investigation completed within 15 business days
Step 6: Written outcome shared with employee within 5 days of investigation completion
Step 7: If unsatisfied, employee may appeal to HR Director within 10 days

ANTI-RETALIATION:
- Raising a grievance will not negatively impact your employment, performance review, or career
- Retaliation against someone who raised a grievance is a serious disciplinary offence
- Any retaliation should be reported immediately to HR Director or Ethics hotline

CONFIDENTIALITY:
- All grievance matters handled with strict confidentiality
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- Witnesses requested to maintain confidentiality during investigation

HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION COMPLAINTS:
- Serious matters (harassment, discrimination, violence) escalated immediately to HR Director
- Can be reported anonymously via Ethics Hotline: ethics@company.com
- Company maintains zero tolerance for harassment and discrimination
- Investigated within 10 business days with interim protective measures if needed

EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP):
- Free confidential counseling available to all employees
- 6 free sessions per year with certified counselor
- Contact HR to access the EAP (contact details kept confidential)
- Topics: Stress, workplace conflict, mental health, personal challenges
