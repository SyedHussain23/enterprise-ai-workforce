IT Policy Document

Topic: Data Classification and Information Security

Details:
All company information is classified and must be handled according to its classification level.

DATA CLASSIFICATION LEVELS:

CONFIDENTIAL (Highest):
- Examples: Client contracts, financial records, employee personal data, proprietary source code, strategic plans, M&A information, board-level communications
- Handling: Encrypted storage and transmission, restricted access on need-to-know, watermarked, no print unless essential
- Sharing: Only to named authorized individuals via encrypted email
- Storage: Encrypted company SharePoint, not personal devices

INTERNAL (Standard):
- Examples: Company policies, internal memos, product roadmaps, meeting minutes, HR policies, internal presentations
- Handling: For employees only, not shared externally without approval
- Sharing: Company email or Teams, not via personal channels
- Storage: Company SharePoint or network drives

PUBLIC:
- Examples: Marketing materials, public website content, press releases, job postings
- Handling: No restrictions — approved for external sharing
- Sharing: Any channel
- Storage: Any location

HOW TO IDENTIFY CLASSIFICATION:
- Documents should be labeled in header/footer: [CONFIDENTIAL], [INTERNAL], [PUBLIC]
- Email: Add classification label in subject line for sensitive emails
- New documents: Apply classification using Microsoft Sensitivity Labels in Office apps (see ribbon bar → Sensitivity)

HANDLING RULES FOR CONFIDENTIAL DATA:
1. Store only in approved encrypted locations
2. Send only via encrypted email (Outlook Encrypt button)
3. Share only with people who have explicit business need
4. Do not leave printed confidential documents unattended
5. Shred all printed confidential documents after use
6. Do not discuss confidential matters in public areas

DATA LOSS PREVENTION (DLP):
- Company email and SharePoint have automated DLP rules
- DLP automatically detects and blocks: Credit card numbers, Emirates ID numbers, passport numbers, salary data in unencrypted outgoing emails
- If DLP blocks your email: Contact IT to review and override if legitimate business need

CLEAN DESK POLICY:
- No confidential documents visible when away from desk
- Lock screen when leaving workstation (Windows: Win+L)
- Shred all printed confidential documents before discarding

PRINTING CONFIDENTIAL DOCUMENTS:
- Use secure printing (print job held until you authenticate at printer with badge)
- Collect prints immediately — do not leave at printer
- Mark printed confidential documents and lock in secure storage when not in use
