IT Policy Document

Topic: Security Incident Response Policy

Details:
All employees must know how to recognize and respond to security incidents to minimize impact.

WHAT IS A SECURITY INCIDENT:
Any event that threatens the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company information or systems:
- Suspected unauthorized access to systems or data
- Malware or ransomware detected or suspected
- Lost or stolen company device
- Phishing attack (clicked link, entered credentials)
- Unexpected password changes or account lockouts
- Large unauthorized data transfer
- System outage of unclear cause

INCIDENT SEVERITY LEVELS:
Level 1 (Critical): Active ransomware, data breach with exfiltration, production system compromise
Level 2 (High): Suspected unauthorized access, stolen device with sensitive data, targeted phishing attack
Level 3 (Medium): Phishing email received (not clicked), unauthorized software installed, minor policy violations
Level 4 (Low): Lost non-sensitive document, forgotten password lockout, minor anomalies

IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (What YOU do):
Step 1: STAY CALM — do not delete evidence or panic
Step 2: STOP — do not continue using the affected system
Step 3: ISOLATE — unplug network cable or disable Wi-Fi on affected device
Step 4: CALL IT SECURITY IMMEDIATELY — Ext. 1001 (24/7 emergency line)
Step 5: DOCUMENT — Write down exactly what happened, when, and any error messages
Step 6: DO NOT POWER OFF — unless IT explicitly instructs you to

REPORTING INCIDENTS:
- During business hours: IT Helpdesk Ext. 1001 or security@company.com
- After hours (critical incidents only): +971-55-XXX-XXXX
- Anonymous reporting: IT Portal → Security → Report Incident (no login required)

WHAT HAPPENS AFTER YOU REPORT:
1. IT Security acknowledges within 15 minutes for Level 1–2 incidents
2. Incident Response Team assembled (IT Security, relevant manager, HR if needed)
3. Containment: Isolate affected systems immediately
4. Investigation: Determine scope, cause, and impact
5. Recovery: Restore systems from backup if needed
6. Post-incident review: Identify root cause and prevent recurrence
7. Notification: UAE Cybersecurity Council notified for significant breaches (legal requirement)

WHAT NOT TO DO:
- Do NOT try to investigate or fix the issue yourself (may destroy evidence)
- Do NOT inform other employees beyond your manager (risk of information spreading incorrectly)
- Do NOT discuss suspected breach on email that may be compromised (use phone)
- Do NOT pay ransomware demands

AFTER AN INCIDENT — EMPLOYEE SUPPORT:
- IT provides briefing on what happened and what you need to do
- Password changes required for affected accounts
- Replacement device issued if original compromised
- IT runs additional security checks on your systems for 30 days post-incident
