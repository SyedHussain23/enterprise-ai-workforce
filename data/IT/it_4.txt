IT Policy Document

Topic: Cybersecurity Policy and Threat Awareness

Details:
All employees share responsibility for protecting company data and systems from cyber threats.

THE THREAT LANDSCAPE:
Common cyber attacks targeting companies:
1. Phishing: Fake emails appearing to be from trusted sources, stealing credentials
2. Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing using personal information about you
3. Ransomware: Malicious software that encrypts your files and demands payment
4. Social Engineering: Manipulating employees to reveal information over phone/chat
5. Business Email Compromise (BEC): Fake emails from "CEO" or "Finance" requesting wire transfers
6. Malware: Malicious software installed through infected attachments or websites

PHISHING RECOGNITION:
Red flags to watch for:
- Email from unknown sender claiming urgency ("Immediate action required")
- Sender domain is slightly wrong: finance@c0mpany.com (notice the zero)
- Generic greetings: "Dear Employee" instead of your name
- Requests for login credentials, passwords, or financial transfers via email
- Unusual links (hover over link to check actual URL before clicking)
- Unexpected attachments (especially .zip, .exe, .docm files)

WHAT TO DO IF YOU RECEIVE A SUSPICIOUS EMAIL:
1. Do NOT click any links or open attachments
2. Do NOT reply to the email
3. Forward to: security@company.com or use "Report Phishing" button in Outlook
4. Delete the email after reporting
5. If you accidentally clicked: Disconnect from internet immediately, call IT: Ext. 1001

PHISHING SIMULATION:
- Company runs monthly phishing simulation exercises
- If you click a simulated phishing email: Mandatory 30-minute security awareness training
- Purpose: Training, not punishment — helps identify where training is needed

DATA PROTECTION RULES:
- Lock your screen when leaving workstation (Windows: Win+L, Mac: Ctrl+Cmd+Q)
- Do not store sensitive data on personal cloud services (Google Drive, Dropbox, iCloud)
- Use company OneDrive/SharePoint for all work files
- Encrypt sensitive files before emailing (use company email encryption — S/MIME)
- Do not print sensitive documents and leave them on desks — shred when done

USB AND REMOVABLE MEDIA:
- Personal USB drives are PROHIBITED on company computers
- Company-issued encrypted USB drives available from IT for approved use only
- USB ports are monitored — unauthorized devices trigger security alert

INTERNET SECURITY:
- Avoid suspicious websites; company firewall blocks known malicious sites
- Do not download software from unofficial sources
- Use only company-approved browser extensions
- Clear browser cache weekly on shared computers

REPORTING SECURITY INCIDENTS:
- Report ANY suspected security incident immediately: it-security@company.com or Ext. 1001
- Signs of compromise: Unusual pop-ups, slow computer, unexpected program launches, unknown files
- Never try to investigate or handle a security incident yourself
