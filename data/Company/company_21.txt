Company Policy Document

Topic: Leadership Standards, Expectations & Development

Details:
What great leadership looks like at our company and how we develop leaders at all levels.

LEADERSHIP PHILOSOPHY:
We believe leadership is not a title — it is a mindset and a set of behaviours. Leaders at every level create the conditions for their teams to do the best work of their lives. We develop leaders who are authentic, inclusive, accountable, and growth-oriented.

OUR LEADERSHIP MODEL — "THE 5 Cs":
1. Clarity — Great leaders give their teams clarity on what success looks like and why it matters
2. Coaching — They develop their people through feedback, challenge, and support (not just telling)
3. Courage — They have difficult conversations, make tough calls, and stand behind their decisions
4. Collaboration — They break down silos and build bridges across teams and departments
5. Character — They lead with integrity, empathy, and consistency; they model the values

LEADERSHIP EXPECTATIONS BY LEVEL:

Team Lead / Senior (Grade 5–6):
- Conduct bi-weekly 1-on-1s with all direct reports
- Set and review SMART goals quarterly
- Actively remove obstacles blocking their team's performance
- Recognise good work promptly and specifically
- Create space for psychological safety — all voices welcomed

Manager / Senior Manager (Grade 7):
- Build a high-performing team culture; manage team dynamics proactively
- Own talent decisions: Hiring, performance management, succession planning
- Develop at least one successor for their own role
- Represent the team's needs upward; represent company direction downward
- Manage budget responsibly; report variances to Finance monthly

Director / VP (Grade 8):
- Set departmental strategy aligned with company vision
- Lead quarterly town halls for their function
- Partner with peers across the C-suite for business outcomes
- Represent company values externally (clients, industry, government)
- Drive Emiratization targets within their function

LEADERSHIP DEVELOPMENT PROGRAMMES:

Management Development Programme (MDP):
- Open to: Grade 5+ employees identified as high-potential future managers
- Duration: 6 months; blended learning (workshops + coaching + project)
- Modules: Leading self, leading teams, stakeholder management, financial acumen, UAE business context
- Executive Sponsor: Assigned C-suite member who mentors each cohort member
- Outcomes: Formal certification; consideration for next promotion cycle

Senior Leadership Programme:
- Open to: Grade 7–8 leaders
- External partner: Top business school (INSEAD, LBS, or equivalent)
- Focus: Strategic leadership, innovation, global business perspective
- Duration: 3–5 days residential or modular virtual delivery
- Fully funded by company; nominated by CHRO

Executive Coaching:
- Available for Grade 8 and selected Grade 7 leaders
- 6–12 sessions with certified external executive coach
- Focuses: Leadership presence, decision-making, managing up/sideways
- Nominated by CHRO in consultation with CEO

360-DEGREE FEEDBACK FOR LEADERS:
- Annual 360 for all Grade 6+: Peers, direct reports, skip-level, and stakeholders
- Results shared in dedicated session with HR Business Partner
- Individual development plan created post-360

LEADERSHIP ACCOUNTABILITY:
- Leaders who consistently underperform on people management KPIs (engagement scores, attrition) are coached and may be subject to HR review
- "Manager Effectiveness Score": Published quarterly — derived from team engagement survey
- Low manager effectiveness: HR-facilitated coaching programme mandatory

MENTORING:
- All Grade 8 leaders expected to mentor 1–2 employees per year (formal mentoring programme)
- All Grade 6–7 encouraged to be mentors (voluntary)
- Mentoring counted in leadership performance review as positive contribution
