Company Policy Document

Topic: Dress Code & Professional Appearance Policy

Details:
The company's dress code standards reflect our professional brand and UAE cultural values.

GENERAL STANDARD: BUSINESS CASUAL
- Business casual is the daily standard for all office-based employees
- Clothing should be clean, pressed, ironed, and in good repair
- Company logo items (branded polo, jacket) are always acceptable

WHAT IS APPROPRIATE:
Men:
- Dress trousers, chinos, or smart jeans (no ripped or distressed denim)
- Collared shirts (polo, button-down), smart T-shirts in plain colours
- Blazers, jackets, sweaters
- Closed-toe shoes or clean loafers; no flip-flops in client-facing areas

Women:
- Smart trousers, skirts (knee-length or longer), tailored dresses
- Blouses, shirts, smart tops with appropriate necklines
- Blazers, cardigans
- Closed-toe or smart open-toe shoes

UAE NATIONAL DRESS:
- National dress (Kandura for Emirati men, Abaya for Emirati women) is always welcomed
- No adaptation required — national dress exceeds our dress code standards

SMART CASUAL DAYS (Thursdays):
- Smart jeans, casual shirts, trainers/sneakers permitted on Thursdays only
- Must still be clean and professional in appearance

FORMAL / CLIENT-FACING OCCASIONS:
- Business formal required for client meetings, board presentations, or management meetings
- Men: Suit or formal trousers + blazer + tie optional
- Women: Formal suit, dress suit, or smart formal dress
- Line managers will communicate formal dress requirements in advance

RAMADAN:
- All employees (regardless of religion) must dress more modestly during Ramadan
- Arms and shoulders must be covered; skirts/dresses must be at knee-length or longer
- Showing respect for the holy month is mandatory for all staff

WHAT IS NOT ACCEPTABLE:
- Ripped, torn, or distressed clothing
- Revealing or excessively tight clothing
- Beachwear, sportswear (unless designated gym/sports activity)
- Offensive slogans, graphics, or imagery on clothing
- Flip-flops or bedroom slippers in client areas
- Overly casual or "loungewear" style clothing

REMOTE WORKING:
- Business casual applies during any video calls or virtual meetings
- Full business formal required for recorded or client-facing video sessions

CONSEQUENCES:
- First instance: Verbal reminder from manager
- Repeat instance: HR formal note on file
- Client-facing violation: Employee may be asked to return home and change before resuming work

UNIFORM / BRANDED CLOTHING:
- Operations and field staff are issued branded uniforms — wearing is mandatory
- Replacement of damaged uniforms: Submit request via HR Portal → Assets → Uniform Request

RELIGIOUS OR MEDICAL ACCOMMODATIONS:
- Employees with religious or medical dress requirements should notify HR
- Reasonable accommodations are made in line with UAE DEI policy
