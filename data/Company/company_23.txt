Topic: UAE Regulatory Compliance & Legal Obligations

Details:
Key UAE laws and regulations that govern our operations and employee obligations.

UAE REGULATORY FRAMEWORK:

1. LABOUR LAW:
- Primary Law: UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 (Labour Relations Law — replaces Law No. 8 of 1980)
- Applies to: All private sector employees in the UAE (onshore)
- Key provisions:
  * Employment contracts mandatory (limited or unlimited term)
  * Probation period: Maximum 6 months
  * Notice period: 30 days (standard); 90 days for Grade 6+
  * Annual leave: 21 days/year (years 1–5); 30 days/year after 5 years of service
  * Maternity leave: 60 days (statutory); Company policy: 90 days
  * Sick leave: 90 days per year (15 paid, 30 half-pay, 45 unpaid)
  * End of Service Gratuity (EOSB): 21 days/year for years 1–5; 30 days/year after 5 years
  * Overtime: 125% for regular overtime; 150% for rest days and public holidays
  * Termination: Without cause requires full notice or payment in lieu + EOSB
  * Article 44 (Immediate Dismissal): Fraud, assault, intoxication, disclosure of secrets, unauthorised absence 20+ days

2. WAGE PROTECTION SYSTEM (WPS):
- UAE Cabinet Resolution No. 46 of 2013
- Mandatory: All salaries paid via bank transfer through UAE WPS system
- Deadline: Salaries must be credited by last working day of each month
- Delays over 10 days: MOHRE can impose sanctions on the company
- Employees paid via: Emirates NBD (company's primary bank)

3. UAE DATA PROTECTION LAW:
- Federal Law No. 45 of 2021 (Personal Data Protection Law — PDPL)
- Data minimisation: Only collect data necessary for employment purposes
- Data retention: Employee data retained for 7 years after end of employment (then securely deleted)
- Employees have right to access, correct, and request deletion of their data
- Data breach: Must be reported to UAE PDPL Authority within 72 hours

4. VAT (VALUE ADDED TAX):
- UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 8 of 2017
- VAT rate: 5% on most goods and services
- Exempt: Healthcare, education, financial services (some)
- Registration threshold: AED 375,000 annual taxable supplies
- All invoices must include: TRN number, VAT amount, supplier/buyer details

5. CORPORATE TAX:
- UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022
- Rate: 9% on taxable profits exceeding AED 375,000
- Effective: Financial years starting on or after 1 June 2023
- Small Business Relief: Companies with revenue under AED 3M may apply for simplified treatment

6. ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING (AML):
- UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018
- All employees must complete AML training annually
- Suspicious transactions reported to Compliance Officer immediately
- Cash transactions over AED 55,000 are prohibited without approval
- KYC (Know Your Customer) mandatory for all new client onboarding

7. ANTI-CORRUPTION & BRIBERY:
- UAE Federal Law No. 6 of 2016
- UAE Penal Code provisions on bribery (Articles 234–239)
- Zero tolerance — see Company Ethics Policy (Company_11)

8. OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH & SAFETY:
- UAE Federal Law No. 8 of 1980 (Article 91–99 — safety obligations)
- Annual fire safety training mandatory for all employees
- Accidents reported to Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE) within 24 hours

9. IMMIGRATION & VISA:
- All non-UAE-national employees must hold a valid UAE residence visa and work permit
- Visa arranged by company at zero cost to employee
- Emirates ID: Mandatory for all UAE residents; company assists with application
- Visa renewal: Managed by HR 60 days before expiry — employee must provide updated documents

10. PRIVACY AT WORK:
- CCTV in public areas of the office (not in prayer rooms, changing rooms, or bathrooms)
- Company devices monitored for security (see IT Policy it_10.txt)
- Personal communications on company devices: Not private

EMPLOYEE OBLIGATIONS:
- Complete all mandatory compliance training on time
- Report any compliance concerns to compliance@company.com immediately
- Do not engage in any activity that could expose the company to regulatory risk
- Keep personal documents (Emirates ID, visa, passport) current; inform HR 30 days before expiry
