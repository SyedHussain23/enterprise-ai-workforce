Company Policy Document

Topic: Performance Management & Appraisal System

Details:
The company's approach to goal-setting, performance reviews, and development planning.

PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT CYCLE:
- Annual cycle: January (goal-setting) → Mid-year review (July) → Year-end review (December)
- All employees participate regardless of grade or tenure
- Performance ratings directly impact salary increases and bonuses

GOAL-SETTING (January):
Framework: SMART Goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Minimum 3, maximum 7 KPIs/OKRs per employee
- 70% business KPIs + 30% behavioural/values competencies
- Goals agreed between employee and manager by 31 January
- Entered into HR Portal → Performance → My Goals
- Mid-year goals may be adjusted by mutual agreement if business priorities shift

PERFORMANCE RATING SCALE (1–5):
1 — Far Below Expectations: Significant performance issues; PIP required
2 — Below Expectations: Partially meets requirements; development plan needed
3 — Meets Expectations: Fully competent; good solid performance
4 — Exceeds Expectations: Consistently delivers above the bar
5 — Outstanding: Exceptional performance; top 10% of organisation

MID-YEAR REVIEW (July):
- 30–60 minute structured conversation between employee and manager
- Progress against goals reviewed; adjustments agreed if needed
- Developmental feedback documented in HR Portal
- No formal rating at mid-year — developmental only

YEAR-END APPRAISAL (December):
Process:
1. Employee Self-Assessment (HR Portal → Performance → Self-Review) — by 1 December
2. Manager Assessment — by 10 December
3. Calibration Session — Department heads normalise ratings across teams
4. One-on-one feedback meeting — manager shares final rating by 20 December
5. Employee acknowledges rating in HR Portal

LINKING PERFORMANCE TO PAY:
- Annual salary increase (effective April): Based on performance rating
  - Rating 5 (Outstanding): 15–20% increase
  - Rating 4 (Exceeds): 10–15% increase
  - Rating 3 (Meets): 5–8% increase
  - Rating 2 (Below): 0–3% increase (review plan required)
  - Rating 1 (Far Below): 0%; PIP triggered
- Year-end bonus: Performance multiplier applied to grade-based bonus (see Finance policy)

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN (PIP):
- Triggered for rating 1 or two consecutive rating 2s
- Duration: 60–90 days structured plan
- Weekly check-ins between employee and manager (HR may attend)
- Clear measurable targets must be met to exit PIP
- Failure to meet PIP targets: Employment may be terminated per UAE Labour Law

CONTINUOUS FEEDBACK:
- 1-on-1 meetings: Bi-weekly minimum between manager and direct report
- Praise and recognition: Use HR Portal → Recognition → Give a Shoutout anytime
- 360-degree feedback: Available on request for employees at Grade 5 and above
- Feedback should be: Specific, Timely, Actionable, and Respectful (STAR model)

APPEAL PROCESS:
- Employee who disagrees with their rating may appeal within 14 days of receiving it
- Submit: HR Portal → Performance → Appeal Rating
- HR reviews appeal with HR Director and relevant department head
- Decision communicated within 21 days; decision is final

CONTACTS:
- HR Portal (performance): https://hr.company.internal → Performance
- Performance queries: hr@company.com
- PIP queries: Contact HR Business Partner for your department
