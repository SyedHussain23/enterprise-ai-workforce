Company Policy Document

Topic: Teamwork, Collaboration & Cross-Functional Working

Details:
How we collaborate effectively across teams, departments, and locations.

COLLABORATION PRINCIPLES:
- We believe the best solutions come from diverse perspectives working together
- Cross-functional collaboration is expected, not optional
- "That's not my job" is not acceptable — we help each other across team boundaries
- Credit is shared — we celebrate team wins, not individual heroics

HOW WE WORK IN TEAMS:

Project Teams:
- Multi-functional project teams formed for initiatives that span departments
- Project Lead assigned by department head; team members nominated by their managers
- Project Kickoff: Scope, timeline, roles, and RACI clearly documented
- Weekly project syncs: 30 minutes; action items tracked in Microsoft Planner
- Project Retrospective: Conducted at end of every project — what went well? What could improve?

Cross-Department Requests:
- Requests between departments: Made via Teams or email to the relevant team lead
- Service Level for internal requests: 2–3 business days for non-urgent; same-day for P1 urgent
- If request is delayed: Escalate to relevant department manager (not HR)

TEAM NORMS:
Decision-Making:
- Consensus preferred; if consensus can't be reached, the team lead decides
- Disagree and commit: Once a decision is made, everyone commits — no sabotage or passive resistance
- Escalate unresolved disputes to department head within 5 business days

Meetings (see also Company_7):
- Meetings have an agenda; only essential attendees invited
- Action items always have an owner and due date
- Decisions documented in meeting minutes

Communication:
- Default to transparency — share information that helps colleagues do their job better
- "Don't surprise your manager" — keep managers updated on risks, issues, or delays
- Communicate early and often — especially on cross-team dependencies

REMOTE & HYBRID TEAM COLLABORATION:
- Virtual-first meeting design: All meetings accessible for both in-office and remote employees
- Async-first culture: Not everything needs a meeting — use Teams channels and SharePoint for updates
- Shared team calendar: All team members keep calendars updated with availability
- Team norms document: Each team maintains a simple "how we work" agreement

CONFLICT RESOLUTION WITHIN TEAMS:
1. Address directly: Speak to the colleague directly; most conflicts resolve with honest conversation
2. Manager involvement: If unresolved after 48 hours, involve the line manager
3. HR involvement: If still unresolved, HR Mediation is available (formally requested via hr@company.com)
4. Formal grievance: See Grievance Policy (HR Document — hr_10.txt)

TEAM BUILDING:
- Annual team offsite / team building event: Budget provided per department
- Monthly informal team lunches: Encouraged (no approval needed for team lunches under AED 500 total)
- Team WhatsApp groups: Allowed for social purposes; maintain professionalism
- Culture Committee organises company-wide social events quarterly

RECOGNISING TEAMWORK:
- Peer recognition: HR Portal → Recognition → Give a Shoutout (visible to whole team)
- Team of the Quarter: Nominated by employees; announced at Town Hall; AED 2,000 team budget reward
- Cross-functional recognition: Specifically recognised in performance reviews as a positive differentiator

CONTACTS:
- Internal collaboration: Microsoft Teams
- Project management: Microsoft Planner
- HR Mediation: hr@company.com
- Culture Committee (team events): culture@company.com
