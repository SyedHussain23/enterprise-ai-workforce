Company Policy Document

Topic: Workplace Health, Safety & Emergency Procedures

Details:
Health and safety standards, emergency procedures, and employee responsibilities in all company offices.

UAE LEGAL FRAMEWORK:
- UAE Federal Law No. 8 of 1980 (Labour Law — occupational health provisions)
- UAE Ministerial Resolution No. 32 of 1982 (occupational safety and health)
- Dubai Civil Defence Fire Safety Regulations
- Company maintains ISO 45001 Occupational Health & Safety Management System

GENERAL SAFETY RESPONSIBILITIES:

Employees Must:
- Follow all safety signs, instructions, and emergency procedures
- Report unsafe conditions, near-misses, or accidents immediately to their manager and Facilities
- Not interfere with safety equipment (fire extinguishers, first aid kits, emergency exits)
- Attend mandatory annual fire safety training (30 minutes online; Facilities team schedules)
- Wear appropriate PPE if in operations, warehouse, or field environments

Management Must:
- Conduct monthly safety walkthrough of their areas
- Ensure new joiners receive safety induction in Week 1
- Report all accidents and incidents within 24 hours to HR and Facilities
- Maintain up-to-date emergency contact list for their team

OFFICE SAFETY STANDARDS:
- All emergency exits must remain unobstructed at all times
- No cooking or open flames in the office (microwave use: kitchen only)
- Electrical cables must not be a trip hazard — report to Facilities if cable management is needed
- Heavy items stored at floor level or on lower shelves only
- No standing on chairs — use step stools/ladders provided by Facilities
- Maximum room capacity signs must be respected in meeting rooms

FIRE SAFETY:
- Fire alarms tested: First Sunday of every month at 10:00 AM (10-second test — do not evacuate)
- Fire drills: Twice per year (unannounced); all employees must participate
- Evacuation assembly point: [Location specific to each office — posted on all floors]
- Fire marshal list: Posted on each floor and on SharePoint → Safety → Fire Marshals

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES:
In Case of Fire:
1. Activate nearest fire alarm pull station
2. Call Dubai Civil Defence: 997 (or local emergency in your emirate)
3. Evacuate immediately via emergency exits — do NOT use elevators
4. Proceed to assembly point; do NOT re-enter building until "all clear" given by Civil Defence
5. Notify your line manager and Facilities immediately after evacuation

Medical Emergency:
1. Call UAE ambulance: 998
2. Notify reception and Facilities: They will meet the ambulance
3. Administer first aid only if trained (first aid kit locations posted on each floor)
4. Do NOT move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger

Security Incident:
1. Call UAE Police: 999
2. Notify security desk and Facilities immediately
3. Follow security team instructions; do not confront intruders

MENTAL HEALTH & WELLBEING:
- EAP (Employee Assistance Programme): 6 free confidential counselling sessions (see HR Policy)
- Stress leave: Covered under sick leave policy — medical certificate required after 2 days
- Ergonomic assessments: Available on request from Facilities for employees with back/posture concerns
- Standing desks available: Request via Facilities → Ergonomic Assessment

INCIDENT REPORTING:
- Report all accidents, near-misses, or unsafe conditions within 24 hours
- Report via: Facilities → Safety → Incident Report (SharePoint form)
- Major accidents (injury requiring medical treatment): Also reported to MOHRE within 24 hours (UAE law requirement)

- Dubai Police: 999
- Dubai Civil Defence: 997
- First Aid Kit Locations: Posted on each floor
