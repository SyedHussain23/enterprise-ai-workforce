Company Policy Document

Topic: Meetings, Collaboration & Communication Standards

Details:
Best practices for running effective meetings and collaborating across teams.

MEETING STANDARDS:

Types of Meetings:
- 1-on-1: Employee and manager (bi-weekly minimum; private, developmental)
- Team Stand-up: Daily 15-minute sync (scrum-style): What did I do? What will I do? Blockers?
- Department Meeting: Weekly 30–60 minutes; structured agenda; action items recorded
- All-Hands / Town Hall: Quarterly; CEO-led; full company; recorded and shared
- Client Meeting: Formal; agenda distributed 48 hours in advance; follow-up minutes within 24 hours
- Project Kickoff / Retrospective: At start and end of each project

BEFORE EVERY MEETING:
- Send calendar invite with agenda at least 24 hours in advance
- Define the meeting objective: Decision? Information sharing? Problem-solving?
- Invite only essential participants — no "just in case" invites
- Share pre-reading materials if required

DURING MEETINGS:
- Start and end on time — always
- Designate a note-taker at the start
- No phones except for emergencies; cameras on for video calls
- One person speaks at a time — respect all contributors
- Decisions and action items captured in real-time
- If a topic runs over: "Parking lot" it for a separate meeting

AFTER MEETINGS:
- Minutes sent within 24 hours (via email or SharePoint document)
- Action items: Owner, deliverable, and due date clearly specified
- Follow up on open action items at next meeting

NO-MEETING ZONES:
- Wednesday: No internal meetings 12:00 PM–2:00 PM (focus time)
- First Sunday of each month: No meetings 9:00 AM–11:00 AM (strategy reflection time)

VIRTUAL MEETING STANDARDS (Microsoft Teams / Zoom):
- Camera on unless bandwidth prevents it (notify team verbally)
- Mute when not speaking to avoid background noise
- Use "raise hand" feature to signal you want to speak
- Meetings recorded only with all participants' consent
- Recordings stored on SharePoint; auto-deleted after 90 days

MEETING TOOLS:
- Internal: Microsoft Teams (primary)
- Client video: Teams or Zoom (client preference)
- Calendar: Microsoft Outlook — all meetings must be in Outlook calendar
- Notes: Microsoft OneNote or SharePoint document library
- Whiteboards: Microsoft Whiteboard (integrated with Teams)

COLLABORATION TOOLS:
- Microsoft Teams: All internal communication, chat, file sharing
- SharePoint: Document management, team sites, knowledge base
- OneDrive: Personal file storage (synced to cloud automatically)
- Outlook: Email (formal communications, external parties)
- Microsoft Planner: Task and project management

MEETING ROOM BOOKING:
- Book via Outlook Calendar → Rooms → Select available room
- Rooms must be booked in advance (walk-in booking is not reliable)
- Cancel bookings you don't need — "ghost rooms" are a waste of space
- Leave meeting rooms clean and tidy; reset AV equipment after use
- Hybrid rooms: Cameras and microphones installed — test before client meetings
